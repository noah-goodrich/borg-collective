## Key Findings

1. **Google's official stance (May 21, 2025 update):** "Using generative AI tools to create
   many pages without adding value for users may violate our spam policy on scaled content
   abuse." AI authorship is not itself penalized; abuse-of-scale without value is.
2. **Quantified effect of pure-AI publishing:** websites relying solely on AI content lost
   an average of 17% of traffic and dropped 8 positions in search rankings. Sites combining
   AI with human oversight dropped only 3 positions and lost 6%.
3. **Grokipedia case:** AI-generated Wikipedia clone gained traction then started losing
   Google visibility in late Jan / early Feb 2025; tracked by multiple SEO commentators.
4. **The penalty mechanism is "scaled content abuse,"** not "AI detection." Sites that
   produce 1,000 thin AI articles a month to chase long-tail keywords are flagged.
5. **Indie hacker implication:** Treating AI as a drafting tool with mandatory human review
   matches the lower-penalty cohort. Pure AI-pipeline content farms are getting punished.
