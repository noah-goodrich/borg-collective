## Key Findings

1. **Definition of Human-Led AI Marketing:** "Humans retain ownership of strategy, creative
   judgment, and final decisions, while AI accelerates research, production, execution, and
   optimization."
2. **Three layers and where humans must own decisions:**
   - **Strategy:** humans define the business problem, set objectives/constraints/trade-offs,
     decide what moves forward. AI accelerates research and scenario exploration.
   - **Creative:** humans own narrative, tone, quality standards, final approvals. AI
     generates drafts, variants, format adaptations.
   - **Execution:** humans approve critical outputs before release; AI manages scale,
     formatting, optimization. Clear escalation points for ambiguity.
3. **Failure modes when HITL is absent:** automation bypassing approvals; disconnected tools
   creating inconsistent outputs; teams unclear on final decision ownership; performance
   insights arriving too late to act on.
4. **"Creativity depends on context. AI can assist, but humans must remain accountable for
   meaning and taste."** Drift, emotionally flat content, and high-output-masking-repetitive
   ideas all flagged as failure patterns.
5. **The pod model groups strategy, creative, analytics, and AI workflow specialists in one
   accountable team** — eliminates handoffs that traditionally hide decision drift.
