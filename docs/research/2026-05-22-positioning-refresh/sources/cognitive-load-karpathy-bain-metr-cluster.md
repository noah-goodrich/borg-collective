## Key Findings

- Developers using AI tools take 19% LONGER to complete tasks than developers without
  (METR study, 2026) — the headline finding that inverts the standard productivity narrative.
- AI coding tools behave "like extremely capable yet highly distractible junior developers"
  (Karpathy) — distractibility is the specific failure mode, not capability.
- The industry "is making too big a jump" (Karpathy) — adoption is outpacing demonstrated
  productivity benefit.
- Bain & Co.'s consulting analysis confirms uneven productivity gains across teams using
  AI coding tools, with senior engineers seeing smaller gains or net losses.
- Implication for borg: the value proposition isn't "more parallel sessions" — it's "fewer,
  better-managed sessions with cognitive-load guardrails."
